Secret Sharing

A secret sharing scheme allows a dealer to share a secret sss among nnn parties P1,P2,…,PnP_1, P_2, \dots, P_nP1​,P2​,…,Pn​ such that any authorized subset of parties can use their shares to reconstruct the secret

  • All other subsets learn nothing about the secret from their shares

This is useful in cases where we need to distribute required trust while also allowing reconstruction even if some of the parties fail

We focus on a common access structure whcih is ttt out of nnn, or threshold secrete sharing

  • Authorized subsets are all those of size at least ttt
t out of n Secret Sharing Scheme

A ttt -out-of-$n$ secret sharing scheme consists of a pair of algorithms:

  • Share is a randomized algorithm that takes as input a messages mmm and outputs a $n$-tuple of shares
  • Reconstruct is a deterministic algorithm that given a ttt -tuple of shares, outputs a message mmm

Two properties have to hold:

  • Correctness: the probability of reconstructing from any subset of size ttt is 111
  • Security: for every m,m′m, m'm,m′ and every subset III of size less than ttt, the distribution of the secrets looks the same for both mmm and m′m'm′

Exponential Families

An exponential family is a parameterized family of distributions

Single-Parameter Exponential Family

For some X⊂RX \subset \realsX⊂R, a parameterized family of distributions over the alphabet YYY is a single parameter exponential family if it can be expressed in the form:
py(y;x)=exp(λ(x)t(y)−α(x)+βy)p_y(y; x) = \t{exp}\p{\lambda(x)t(y) - \alpha(x) + \beta{y}}py​(y;x)=exp(λ(x)t(y)−α(x)+βy)
for some choice of functions λ,t,β\lambda, t, \betaλ,t,β

  • λ\lambdaλ: natural parameter
  • ttt: natural statistic
  • β\betaβ: log base function

α\alphaα is not part of the specification because it is for normalization

  • A family may become no longer possible to be normalized for specific xxx

Z(x)=eα(x)=∑yexp(λ(x)t(y)+β(y))Z(x) = e^{\alpha(x)} = \sum_y \t{exp}\p{\lambda(x) t(y) + \beta(y)}Z(x)=eα(x)=y∑​exp(λ(x)t(y)+β(y))

  • Z(x)Z(x)Z(x) is known as the partition function
  • α(x)\alpha(x)α(x) is then known as the log-partition function

We denote that yyy follows an exponential family by:
y∼p∈E(X,Y,λ,t,β)y \sim p \in \mc{E}(\mc{X}, \mc{Y}, \lambda, t, \beta)y∼p∈E(X,Y,λ,t,β)

We note that if we instead factor out β(y)\beta(y)β(y) to get:
py(y;x)∝q(y)exp(λ(x)t(y))p_y(y; x) \propto q(y) \t{exp}\p{\lambda(x)t(y)}py​(y;x)∝q(y)exp(λ(x)t(y))

  • If we can get q(y)q(y)q(y) to be a distribution (integrate to $1$) then this is known as the base distribution of the family
Canonical Single-Parameter Exponential Family

A canonical exponential family is one with λ(x)=x\lambda(x) = xλ(x)=x

Natural Parameter Set

The natural parameter space is the collection of all possible xxx such that the corresponding form of the distribution can be normalized

  • This is a convex set, which we will see shortly
  • When Y\mc{Y}Y is finite, it is an open set
Natural Exponential Families

Canonical exponential families with Y⊂R\mc{Y} \subset \realsY⊂R and t(y)=yt(y) = yt(y)=y are referred to as natural exponential families

Property 1

The log-partition function for a general exponential family satisfies:

Property 2

The log-partition function for a general exponential family satisfies:
d2dx2α(x)=(ddxλ(x))2⋅d2dx2λ(x)⋅Var[t(y)]\frac{d^2}{dx^2} \alpha(x) = \p{\deriv{}{x} \lambda(x)}^2 \cdot \frac{d^2}{dx^2} \lambda(x) \cdot \var{t(y)}dx2d2​α(x)=(dxd​λ(x))2⋅dx2d2​λ(x)⋅Var[t(y)]

Exponential Family Constructions and Interpretations

Many familiar parameterized distributions can be expressed as exponential families:

  • Bernoulli random variable
  • Binomial random variable
  • Guassian random variable
  • Exponential random variable

The uniform random variable is not exponential

Categorizing and Reducing Exponential Families

For a family of distributions, in order for yyy to be useful in estimating xxx, the distribution py(⋅,x)p_y(\cdot, x)py​(⋅,x) must be associated with a unique value of xxx

  • In this case, we say the parameter is identifiable from yyy in family
  • These are the only families that are of interest
  • Formally, this means that there does not exist a x1x_1x1​ and x2x_2x2​ s.t. x1≠x2x_1 \neq x_2x1​=x2​ and py(⋅;x1)=py(⋅;x2)p_y(\cdot; x_1) = p_y(\cdot; x_2)py​(⋅;x1​)=py​(⋅;x2​)

Minimal Families

Identifiable exponential families may be reducible:

  • Consider λ(x)=[x,2x−1]\lambda(x) = \bracket{x, 2x-1}λ(x)=[x,2x−1]
  • We have ln⁡py(y;x)=xt1(y)+(2x−1)t2(y)−α(x)+β(y)\ln p_y(y; x) = x t_1(y) + (2x-1)t_2(y) - \alpha(x) + \beta(y)lnpy​(y;x)=xt1​(y)+(2x−1)t2​(y)−α(x)+β(y)
    • This can be equivalently exxpressed as x(t1(y)−2t2(y))−α(x)+(β(y)−t2(y))x(t_1(y) - 2t_2(y)) - \alpha(x) + (\beta(y) - t_2(y))x(t1​(y)−2t2​(y))−α(x)+(β(y)−t2​(y)) which is an exponential family of smaller dimension
  • This is known as reducing the exponential family
Reducibility of Exponential Families

Any exponential family of dimension K≥2K \geq 2K≥2 that has the natural parameters not satisfying any affine constraint cannot be reduced. In fact, any exponential family whose natural parameters satisfy an affine constraint can be expressed as one of dimension K−1K - 1K−1

Some minimal families may satisfy non-linear constraints, which results in the natural parameter space not being an open subset of RK\reals^KRK

  • A full rank exponential family is a minimal exponential family whose parameter space for λ\lambdaλ includes an open, nonempty set in RK\reals^KRK
  • When a minimal exponential family is not full-rank, it is curved

Inference

One thing to note is that when computing the probability distribution py(y;x)p_y(y; x)py​(y;x), the data yyy participates only through the natural statistics t(y)t(y)t(y) and β(y)\beta(y)β(y)

  • Once we compute these K+1K + 1K+1 numbers, we can throw away the data and just use these statistics to perform inference on xxx
  • In fact, in exponential families, we don't even need β(y)\beta(y)β(y)
  • To take the MLE estimator, we just need to maximize ln⁡py(y;x)\ln p_y(y; x)lnpy​(y;x) across xxx, and since β(y)\beta(y)β(y) is just an additive term, we can get away with not even computing it

This is an example of sufficient statistics, which are functions of the data that are "loss-free" representations for the purpose of inference

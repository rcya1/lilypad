Bayesian Method

  • In the Bayesian world, we first have aprior pdf f(θ)f(\theta)f(θ) which indicates our prior belief about θ\thetaθ before seeing the data
  • After seeing the data, we update it into a posterior belief
    P(θ∣data)=P(data∣θ)P(θ)P(data)P(\theta | \text{data}) = \frac{P(\text{data} | \theta)P(\theta)}{P(\text{data})}P(θ∣data)=P(data)P(data∣θ)P(θ)​
  • The numerator is just Ln(θ)∗f(θ)L_n(\theta) * f(\theta)Ln​(θ)∗f(θ) and the bottom is a normalization constant that does not depend on θ\thetaθ
  • Therefore, we write our posterior distribution as:
    f(θ∣data)∝Ln(θ)f(θ)f(\theta | \text{data}) \propto L_n(\theta) f(\theta)f(θ∣data)∝Ln​(θ)f(θ)
  • If the prior and posterior turn out to be in the same family of distributions, we call the prior a conjugate prior

Bayes Estimator

Bayes Estimator

The Bayes estimator is the mean of the posterior, or mean a posteriori
θ^Bayes=∫θ⋅f(θ∣data)dθ\hat{\theta}^\text{Bayes} = \int \theta \cdot f(\theta | \text{data}) d\thetaθ^Bayes=∫θ⋅f(θ∣data)dθ

This can be hard to compute explicitly because it requires knowing the normalizing constant

  • Can approximately compute it using Markov Chain Monte Carlo
    • Markov Chain: draw θ1,…,θT\theta_1, \dots, \theta_Tθ1​,…,θT​ approximately iid from f(θ∣X1,…,Xn)f(\theta | X_1, \dots, X_n)f(θ∣X1​,…,Xn​) by simulating a Markov chain
      • This can be done through something like Metropolis Hastings, which requires only the ratio between states, not the actual probability value of the state
    • Monte Carlo: compute the mean of the θi\theta_iθi​ drawn

MAP (mode)

MAP

The maximum a posteriori, or MAP, is the mode of the posterior:
θ^MAP=arg max⁡θf(θ∣data)\hat{\theta}^\text{MAP} = \argmax_\theta f(\theta | \text{data})θ^MAP=θargmax​f(θ∣data)

The mode and mean do not necessarily have to be close (i.e. think about a bimodal posterior)

Binary Hypothesis Testing

In the binary case, the model consists of:

  • Prior probabilities P0,P1P_0, P_1P0​,P1​
  • Likelihood functions py∣H(y∣H0),py∣H(y∣H1)p_{y|H}(y|H_0), p_{y|H}(y|H_1)py∣H​(y∣H0​),py∣H​(y∣H1​)

The solution to this hypothesis test is specified as a decision rule, or a classifier

  • It maps every possible observation yyy to one of the two hypotheses
  • It partitions the observation space into disjoint decision regions

To quantify what is the best, we use an objective function that defines the loss / cost

  • C(Hj,Hi)=CijC(H_j, H_i) = C_{ij}C(Hj​,Hi​)=Cij​
  • Denotes the cost of deciding the hypothesis is HiH_iHi​ when the correct is HjH_jHj​
  • We denote the true hypothesis as HHH and estimated as fff
  • Our goal is then to find the hypothesis that minimizes the cost ϕ(f)\phi(f)ϕ(f)
    • ϕ(f)=E[C(H,f(y))]\phi(f) = \exp{C(H, f(y))}ϕ(f)=E[C(H,f(y))]

    • Expectation is takenover random yyy and HHH
  • The costs CijC_{ij}Cij​ are valid if the cost of a correct decision is lower than that of an incorrect decision
